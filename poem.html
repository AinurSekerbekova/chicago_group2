The Chicago Fire

By the eyes of Horace White

It was too vast, too swift,
The fire that swept the night.
Dreams vanished in the drift,
Lost in the burning light.

Through ashes hope would rise,
From embers life would grow.
Beneath the smoky skies,
Courage began to show.

The city found its dawn,
The flames could not erase.
Chicago, battered, drawn,
Still stood with strength and grace.
