Research Overview

This section explains the research foundations of the Chicago Fire digital heritage project — its question, conceptual answer, mechanism, and core insight.

"How can interactive digital storytelling using survivor narratives reshape public understanding and emotional engagement with historical urban disasters?"

Through embodied, multi-perspective, emotionally-resonant participation in authentic survivor experiences that progressively builds:

  • Systems-level understanding (not facts)
  • Durable emotional investment (not intellectual interest)
  • Civic responsibility (not passive consumption)

  • Player begins in modern Chicago (familiar ground)
  • Discovers survivor testimony at specific 1871 locations (spatial continuity)
  • Hears survivor’s voice describing their experience (emotional authenticity)
  • Makes interactive choice within that experience (agency + stakes)
  • Collects actual historical letter (archive preservation)
  • Repeats with six other survivors (multi-perspective systems thinking)
  • Participates in rebuilding (forward-looking hope)
  • Leaves game fundamentally transformed in understanding and care

Historical understanding and emotional engagement are not passive absorption of facts, but active co-creation of meaning through interactive participation with authentic survivor voices in spatially and temporally meaningful contexts.
