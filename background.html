Project Info

This page contains all the background about the game, including why it is a digital heritage project.

Dimension 1 — Social Impact: The Great Chicago Fire (October 1871) killed 300 people, left 90,000 homeless, and destroyed $200 million in property...

Dimension 2 — Available Evidence: Seven first-hand letters from survivors written during/immediately after the fire provide authentic voices...

  • Jonas Hutchinson (lawyer) → institutional collapse
  • Mary Fales (lawyer’s wife) → family displacement
  • George Howland (school principal) → loss of knowledge
  • Aurelia R. King (merchant’s family) → survival and faith
  • Anna E. Higginson (real estate broker’s wife) → preservation consciousness
  • Adeline Rossiter Judd (volunteer helper) → mutual aid
  • Samuel Stone (assistant librarian) → vision for rebuilding

Dimension 3 — Geographic Specificity: Mapping seven physical locations in modern Chicago...

Dimension 4 — Narrative Power: The story moves from destruction → survival → rebuilding...

Dimension 5 — Audience Engagement: Four narrator-guides + interactive gameplay mechanics...

Step 1 — Conceptualization (Design): Research question: “How can interactive digital storytelling using survivor narratives reshape public understanding...”

Step 2 — Digital Tool Selection: Narrative design, geospatial mapping, and game development using QGIS, ArcGIS, Visual Studio Code...

Step 3 — Implementation: GitHub repo structure (game engine + narrative content + geospatial data + 3D assets)...

Step 4 — Heritage Integration: Links to Chicago History Museum’s digital archive and educational deployment.

  1. Preservation Against Decay: Digital copies protect against fire, flood, and degradation.
  2. Global Accessibility: Makes Chicago’s history meaningful across borders.
  3. Enhanced Interpretation: Immersive storytelling brings static text to life.
  4. Intangible Heritage: Revives the oral storytelling tradition.
  5. Educational Reuse: Enables repurposing for documentaries, research, and museum exhibits.
